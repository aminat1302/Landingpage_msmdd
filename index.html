Discover our Avatars

Pikachu

Pikachu is an Electric-type Pokémon introduced in Generation I. It is well known as the mascot of the Pokémon franchise. Pikachu can generate electric currents with its cheeks and store electricity in its tail.

Charmander

Charmander is a Fire-type Pokémon introduced in Generation I. It has a flame burning on the tip of its tail, which indicates its life force. Charmander evolves into Charmeleon.

Squirtle

Squirtle is a Water-type Pokémon introduced in Generation I. It is a small, light-blue Pokémon with an appearance similar to a turtle. Squirtle evolves into Wartortle.

Bulbasaur

Bulbasaur is a Grass/Poison-type Pokémon introduced in Generation I. It is the first Pokémon in the National Pokédex. Bulbasaur has a plant bulb on its back from birth. The bulb grows as it absorbs sunlight, and it eventually evolves into Ivysaur.

Purchasing Pokémon Cards

Pokémon cards are popular collectible items among Pokémon fans. They depict various Pokémon with unique illustrations and are used in the Pokémon Trading Card Game (TCG). Collectors can purchase packs of Pokémon cards at game stores, collectible card shops, and online.

Characteristics of Pokémon Cards

Pokémon cards are divided into different categories, including basic cards, evolved cards, trainer cards, and energy cards. Each card has features such as its name, type, illustration, hit points (HP), attacks, and weaknesses. Some cards may also have special effects or abilities.
